Organic farming helps improve fertility of the soil

20 june 2020 by Jessica 2784
  • Share on Facebook
  • Tweet on Twitter

Soil is a farmer’s greatest asset and is vital inn producing healthy crops. Feed the soil to feed the plant is one of the fundamentals in organic farming. Organic farming is a way to treat soil and plants naturally. Organic farming is a healthier agricultural system that enhances soil fertility. The use of synthetic fertilizers is prohibited in organic farming. The method of rotating crops can stabilize soils and improve water retention. Organic farming uses farm manure, blood and bone meal, vermicompost, sawdust, urine, wood- ash, peat and minerals like limestone, gypsum, sulfur, clay, etc. Another approach of farmers in organic farming is legume crop cultivation to ensure balanced nitrogen supply to the crops. Farmers do not remove soil from the farm as organic farming tries to keep up living soil and improve the soil condition.

Instead of just supplying nutrients through fertilizers, organic farming systems aim in maintaining nutrients in organic reservoirs or in mineral forms. This can be achieved by cycling nutrients through organic reservoirs. By managing organic matter, soil fertility is improved.

A group of interrelated practices improves and maintains chemical, physical and biological properties of soil. A healthy soil is maintained in organic farming through balance nutrient supply. The crops are selected according to climatic conditions, soil conditions and resistance to pests and diseases. In some conditions, farmers use preparations of sodium hydroxide, potassium hydroxide, lime, etc. to control pests and other problems. This does not harm the soil fertility. On the other hand, thermal sterilization is forbidden in organic farming. Crop rotation is a good method to increase soil fertility rate as it increases diversity and is healthy for the soil.

Methods employed in organic farming to improve soil fertility-

  • Cover crops- Since there’s no uses of artificial fertilizers in organic farming, cover crops are an honest thanks to maintain healthy soil. Cover crops improve the organic matter levels in the soil; provide a crop canopy to prevent soil erosion. The soil thus holds the nutrients and enhances nutrient and water content. Farmers use mustard, radish, legumes that fix nitrogen. The legumes overcome nitrogen deficiency within the soil.
  • Crop rotation- Crop rotation is a major way employed by farmers to maintain soil fertility. It prevents nutrient exhaustion and helps to manage weeds, pests and disease problems. Every present nutrient in the soil is thus utilized and soil becomes healthy.
  • Monitoring the soil- Farmers monitor measure and manage the soil health thoroughly so that the soil remains healthy.
  • Avoiding non- point sources- Organic farming avoids creating non-point source (NPS) pollution through surface runoff and leaching. It does not use excess nutrients (especially nitrogen and phosphorus) as they can degrade the quality of soil. This can cause low oxygen levels in the soil i.e. eutrophication. The weeds can thus come into play and damage the crops and soil.
  • Increase soil organic matter- Farmers maintain or increase soil organic matter (SOM) levels through compost. SOM features a high capacity to carry and release inorganic nitrogen and other essential nutrients to plants. Organic matter inputs enhance the steadiness of soil aggregates, makes the soil porous and permeable to water and air. It improves the water-holding capacity of soils. SOM are organic compounds rich in carbon. Building or maintaining the extent of soil carbon provides the energy and nutrients necessary to stimulate the soil microorganisms answerable for decomposition and the formation of soil aggregates and more desirable soil structure.

Organic farming thus improves soil fertility and is establishing itself as a healthier way to switch to.
